SNUB — Community Guidelines

Last updated: October 2025
These guidelines help keep SNUB a safe, anonymous, and fun place to share confessions. They explain what’s allowed, what’s not, and how moderation works.

Our goal

SNUB is a location-based anonymous platform for sharing thoughts, feelings and short confessions. We value safety, respect, and local community trust. These guidelines are here to protect users and make SNUB enjoyable for everyone.

Core rules — what is NOT allowed

  • Hate speech & slurs: No content that attacks or dehumanizes people based on race, religion, caste, gender, sexual orientation, disability, nationality, or any protected characteristic.
  • Harassment & threats: No threats, doxxing, stalking, or targeted harassment of any person or group.
  • Sexual content involving minors: Zero tolerance — any sexual content involving minors will be removed and reported.
  • Graphic sexual content & pornography: Not allowed in posts or profile media.
  • Encouraging self-harm or suicide: Posts that encourage or describe self-harm, suicide instructions, or glorify harm will be removed and may prompt an emergency response.
  • Illicit activity & instructions: No posts that provide instructions for criminal activity, violent wrongdoing, or drug manufacture.
  • Doxxing & privacy invasion: Sharing someone’s personal, private, or identifying information without consent is strictly forbidden.
  • Impersonation intended to deceive: Posts claiming to be a real person or public figure with intent to mislead that cause harm are prohibited.
  • Spam & bots: Posting repetitive, commercial, promotional content or using automated accounts to manipulate visibility is not allowed.

Allowed content (examples)

  • Anonymous personal experiences, confessions, and feelings that don’t target a person or group.
  • Funny, embarrassing, or awkward stories (that do not involve hate, threats, or sexual content with minors).
  • Questions, open confessions, and local observations that invite empathy and conversation.
Example — Allowed:
“I cheated on my exam and feel guilty — anyone else?”
Example — Not allowed:
“I know where [person] lives — go do X to them” (doxxing / threat)

How moderation works

We use a layered approach:

  • Automated filters: Immediate screening using profanity lists and AI moderation models (multi-language, including Tamil).
  • Community reporting: Users can report posts; multiple reports increase priority for removal.
  • Manual review: Our moderation team reviews flagged content and makes final decisions.
  • Reputation signals: Accounts with repeated violations may be rate-limited, shadow-banned, or suspended.

What happens when content is flagged

  • Low severity: post may be hidden or labelled (e.g., “under review”).
  • High severity: post is removed immediately and account may be suspended.
  • Repeated or severe violations: account suspension or termination; in extreme cases we may cooperate with law enforcement.

Reporting a post

To report a confession, tap the report button on the post and select a reason (Harassment, Hate, Sexual, Self-harm, Spam, Other). Provide any helpful details. Reports are anonymous.

Appeals & support

If your post was removed or you were suspended, you can appeal by contacting our support team. Include:

  • Your user ID (example: abc@snub.app)
  • Post ID or screenshot
  • Short explanation of why you believe the removal was incorrect

Contact: support@snub.app

Safety resources

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or a medical emergency, contact local emergency services right away. For self-harm support, please reach out to local hotlines or trusted people. SNUB is not a crisis service.

Enforcement examples

Warning

First-time minor language/insult → post removed + warning.

Temporary suspension

Repeated harassment or hate content → 7–30 day suspension.

Account termination

Severe violations (doxxing, sexual content with minors, threats) → permanent ban and possible law enforcement reporting.

Privacy & anonymity

While posts are anonymous to other users, SNUB retains internal identifiers (user IDs) to manage abuse and safety. We will not reveal personal identity publicly unless legally compelled.

Tips for a healthy community

  • Be kind — assume others are human beings with feelings.
  • Report harmful content rather than engaging with it.
  • Use the mood tags honestly to help others find relatable posts.
  • Encourage empathy — positive interactions create better engagement.

Changes to these guidelines

We will update the Community Guidelines as SNUB grows. The date at the top shows the last revision. Major changes will be communicated in-app when possible.
